## Board Pitch

**Thesis (1 sentence):**
Agent Trainer is the only AI simulation platform purpose-built for contact centers, combining multilingual voice emulation with proprietary skill evaluation to cut agent ramp time before they ever take a real call.

**The case:**
1. Why now:Contact centers face 30–45% annual agent turnover — training at scale is a burning, unsolved problem. Foundation models now make realistic customer simulation economically viable. Genesys, NICE, and Five9 have no native simulation capability yet. There is a 12–18 month window before they ship one.
2. What's defensible: 0+ languages and accent simulation no competitor matches at launch. A consulting layer — BA-led onboarding and CSM coverage — that creates high switching cost through custom scenario libraries and skill frameworks built over months. 50+ customers with deep configurations not easily portable. Vertical scenario libraries in Horizon 3 will make the product itself the moat.
3. The economics: $15,000 onboarding fee is margin-positive and funds the BA. Recurring COGS sits at $29–46/user/month (Claude + ElevenLabs + CSM). License pricing must clear $60–70/agent/month for healthy margin. Margin improves as native scenario generation reduces BA time, session tier pricing protects against heavy users, and vertical libraries reduce onboarding cost per customer.

**The risks:**
1. Trust / failure modes: AI evaluation scores with no override path creates a reliability gap — a wrong score has no recourse today.
2. Scale / governance:
3. ElevenLabs is simultaneously our core differentiator and our single point of failure on voice. I
4. Competitive: Genesys or NICE ships native simulation within 12–18 months. Our defense is multilingual depth, the consulting layer, and vertical scenario libraries — none of which a platform replicates at launch. We need to be embedded deeply enough before they ship that switching becomes painful.

**The ask:**
Fund Horizon 2 and 3 — native scenario generation, LMS/CRM integrations, and in-platform analytics and  Vertical scenario libraries. These initiatives deepen switching cost, reduce unit economics on onboarding, and turn potential platform attackers into distribution partners before they become competitors.
